Technical debt is being discussed so many times. The decision to quickly put out a feature or an entire project and sacrifice code quality happens often and has its value. A lot has been written about it. You compare it to financial debt, with its pros and cons. 

While one can argue about good and bad debt in a broader way I want to think about good financial and technical decisions on a daily basis. Deciding to take a loan or spend 2 weeks on repaying your technical debt are big decisions. They involve multiple stake holders, communication and mutual agreement.

On the other hand, the day to day financial decisions do not involve a lot of people, mostly only myself. I can decide what the right thing to do is in the moment. I can decide to have takeaway food or to cook at home, to buy a gadget or to leave it there.
